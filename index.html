Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sell a property while it's in probate? +
Yes — in many cases you can sell an inherited property or your ownership interest even before probate is completed. D. Taylor and his probate attorney can explain exactly how this works for your specific situation, at no cost to you.
What if family members don't agree on what to do? +
You may still be able to sell your individual ownership interest even if other heirs don't agree. D. Taylor has helped many families navigate these disputes. Schedule a call to discuss your options — no pressure, no obligation.
What if the executor isn't doing their job? +
If an executor is unresponsive or not fulfilling their duties, you have legal options. Our probate attorney can help you understand your rights and how to move the process forward. The first consultation is free.
What is probate and why does it take so long? +
Probate is the legal process of transferring property after someone passes away. It involves paperwork, court filings, and legal steps that can feel confusing and take months or even years. With the right help, the process can move much faster — in some cases, we close in just a few weeks.
What does your service cost? +
Our consultation and cash offer are 100% free with no obligation. If you sell your property to us, we cover all closing costs — no commissions or fees taken from your proceeds. The attorney consultation is also provided at no upfront cost.
What if the estate is in Florida but I live in another state? +
Not a problem — we handle out-of-state heirs regularly. Everything can be managed remotely. You don't need to travel or be present to sell or navigate the probate process with our help.
What if I just want to talk and learn about my options? +
That's perfectly fine — and encouraged. Even if you're not ready to sell or make any decisions, we'll answer your questions, give you real information, and give you space to make the best decision for you and your family. No pressure whatsoever.
